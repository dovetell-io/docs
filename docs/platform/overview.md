title: Platform overview
description: Planned dovetell platform direction for teams that want to move beyond static templates.

Status: planned.

The first useful dovetell experience can be documentation, templates, and an
assessment. A later platform should exist only where those pieces show repeated
friction that a product can reduce.

The platform direction is to help teams manage context governance as an ongoing
practice instead of a one-time setup.

## Why a platform may be useful

Docs and templates can teach the operating model.

A platform can help when teams need to:

- see which projects have usable context
- review AI-generated or AI-assisted documentation changes
- track whether assessment results improve over time
- manage reusable templates across teams
- preserve decisions and provenance across tools
- route lightweight tasks to the right actor or mode

## Relationship to the assessment

The assessment is the practical entry point.

It identifies the team's current maturity, recommends a next step, and creates a
project trail that can improve over time. As patterns emerge across assessment
results, the platform can focus on problems that are observed rather than
imagined.

## Relationship to templates

Templates are the bridge between diagnosis and behavior change.

The platform should support templates by helping teams:

- find the right template for their maturity level
- adapt a template without losing provenance
- understand whether a template improved the workflow
- keep paid templates separate from public guidance where needed

## Design constraint

Do not build a platform just to house the documentation.

The documentation should remain useful on its own. The platform should earn its
place by making repeated team workflows easier, clearer, or more measurable.
