The dovetell assessment is the first entry point into context governance.

It gives a team a fast read on how systematically they use AI today, where
context breaks down, and which habits or templates are worth adopting next.

It also creates a useful product signal loop. As teams retake the assessment
over time, dovetell can learn which practices improve, which gaps persist, and
which template or platform capabilities should exist next.

## What the assessment does

The assessment asks 30 questions across six capability areas.

The output is:

- a maturity level
- a score out of 90
- a breakdown by capability area
- strengths
- gaps
- recommended next steps
- a unique project link for tracking progress over time

## Why it matters

The assessment is not only a lead magnet or scorecard.

It is a structured way to identify context debt:

- where team knowledge is scattered
- where prompts are not reusable
- where handoffs are inconsistent
- where AI outputs lack review
- where workflow integration is uneven

That makes it a natural path into templates first, and later into a SaaS
product that helps teams manage the context itself.

## Product bridge

The assessment can become the front door for future platform adoption:

1. Diagnose the team's current context maturity.
2. Recommend the lightest useful next step.
3. Provide templates or setup help for immediate improvement.
4. Track improvement over time through retakes.
5. Surface recurring gaps that justify platform capabilities.

This keeps the product grounded in observed team behavior instead of abstract
platform ambition.
