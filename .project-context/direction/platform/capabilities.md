Status: planned.

The future platform should grow out of repeated documentation, assessment, and
template workflows.

## Candidate capabilities

| Capability | Why it matters |
| --- | --- |
| Assessment history | Shows whether a team improves over time. |
| Template recommendations | Connects diagnosis to practical next steps. |
| Context inventory | Shows which projects have usable operating context. |
| Provenance tracking | Preserves who changed what, in which mode, and why. |
| Review queue | Helps teams promote draft context safely. |
| Package access | Supports free and paid template distribution. |
| Decision log | Keeps architectural and workflow decisions visible. |
| Metrics | Shows adoption, gaps, and improvement patterns. |

## Signals before building

A capability is more likely to deserve product investment when:

- multiple teams hit the same friction
- the friction appears in assessment results
- the same template needs repeated adaptation
- manual review becomes the bottleneck
- GitHub or docs-only workflows are no longer enough

## Avoid for now

These may be tempting, but they are probably premature:

- complex role hierarchies
- heavy approval workflows
- broad integrations before the core loop is proven
- generic knowledge-base features
- dashboards without clear decisions attached to them

## Near-term product thesis

Start with the assessment, use templates to create immediate value, and let the
improvement data point toward the platform.
